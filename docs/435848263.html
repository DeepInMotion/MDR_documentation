Deep In Motion : 11.3: Quantitative Classification Criteria for Labeling

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

Requirements

  • This procedure specifies quantitative classification criteria for labeling. The selection of these criteria has been justified by the manufacturer.

Summary

Quantitative classification criteria were defined to ensure that labeling in supervised learning is consistent, objective, and clinically valid.

Classification Criteria

  • CP Diagnosis: Confirmed by standardized neurological assessment at clinical follow-up (e.g., Gross Motor Function Classification System, GMFCS).

  • Timepoint of Confirmation: Labels assigned only after diagnostic confirmation at or beyond 24 months corrected age, when CP diagnosis is considered reliable.

  • Binary Labeling Rule:

    • CP: Infant meets established diagnostic criteria for cerebral palsy.

    • Non-CP: Infant does not meet criteria at the confirmed timepoint.

  • Handling of Ambiguity: Cases without sufficient follow-up or with uncertain outcomes were excluded from labeling.

Justification of Criteria

  • Clinical Relevance: Criteria directly reflect the established clinical gold standard for CP diagnosis.

  • Objectivity: Use of quantitative, validated scales ensures labels are reproducible and not dependent on subjective assessment.

  • Regulatory Consistency: Criteria correspond directly to the intended use and diagnostic standards, fulfilling MDR requirements for transparent and justified labeling procedures.
