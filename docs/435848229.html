Deep In Motion : 10.9: Data Protection

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

A formal procedure to ensure compliance with applicable data protection regulations, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and corresponding national laws is implemented.

Procedure

  • Informed Consent

    • Written consent obtained from parents or legal guardians prior to data collection.

    • Consent forms included clear information on purpose, storage, use, and rights to withdraw.

  • Data Anonymization / Pseudonymization

    • All video files and associated clinical records were pseudonymized prior to analysis.

    • Direct identifiers (name, hospital ID, date of birth) were removed and replaced with coded identifiers.

  • Secure Storage and Transfer

    • Data stored on encrypted servers with controlled access.

    • Transfers between study sites and the central repository followed secure, encrypted channels.

  • Access Control

    • Role-based access ensured that only authorized personnel could view or process data.

    • Access logs maintained for audit purposes.

  • Retention and Deletion Policy

    • Data retained only for the duration necessary to complete development, external validation, and regulatory submission.

    • Procedures for permanent deletion after expiration of retention period are in place.

Justification

  • Protects the rights and privacy of infants and families.

  • Ensures compliance with legal and ethical requirements.

  • Provides traceable safeguards in line with MDR expectations for handling clinical data.
