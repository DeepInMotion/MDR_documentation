Deep In Motion : 15.5 Training and Comparison

Created by Felix Tempel, last updated on 17.06.2025

Within the project, three ML approaches have been developed (Tempel et al.):


Interpretability Considerations

  • The NAS model is favored for deployment due to its simplicity and suitability for integration with explainability tools, since it is only one model.

  • The Ensemble is harder to interpret, since the explanability output is averaged over the 70 models.
  • The Conventional ML approach is inferior to all three other methods.

Model Selection Justification
The Ensemble model was selected as the final model due to:

  • The model was already published
  • Future research should look into the single model from Tempel et al., when working with resource-restricted environments, and when explanability is important

Comments

  • All models were trained and validated using the same data splits and evaluation metrics for fairness.

  • Simpler models were not only tested but also documented for future use in resource-limited clinical environments.

  • The final decision favors models that maintain regulatory alignment while meeting technical and clinical usability thresholds.

Regulatory References

  • ISO 13485: Design validation procedures require assessment of alternative solutions.

  • AI-specific standards (e.g., ISO/IEC TR 24028): Encourage model transparency, resource efficiency, and interpretability.
