Deep In Motion : 10.6: Justification of Data Collection and Representativeness

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

The origin of data is specified and their representativeness for the intended target population justified. Data were collected from 13 hospitals across Belgium, India, Norway, and the United States, covering diverse healthcare settings and patient demographics.

Justification of Representativeness

  • Geographic and Demographic Coverage: Inclusion of multiple international sites ensures variability in ethnicity, socioeconomic background, and clinical practice, reflecting the diversity of the target population.

  • Clinical Relevance: All infants were assessed within the clinically relevant age range (9â€“18 weeks corrected age), corresponding to the established window for general movement assessment.

  • Comparative Benchmarking: Baseline characteristics (sex distribution, gestational age, birth weight etc.) were compared to values reported in peer-reviewed studies and neonatal registers. No material deviations were identified.

  • Outcome Prevalence: The observed cerebral palsy prevalence (~15%) is consistent with reported rates in high-risk neonatal cohorts, supporting the external validity of the sample.

Justification Against Literature and Registers

  • Published epidemiological data confirm that the study population aligns with high-risk infant cohorts typically used for early cerebral palsy screening.

  • Comparison with neonatal registers demonstrates consistency in demographic and clinical attributes, supporting generalizability.
