Deep In Motion : 11.1: Derivation and Justification of Labels in Supervised Learning

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

For supervised learning of the CP prediction method, labels were derived in direct alignment with the device’s intended use: early detection of cerebral palsy (CP) in high-risk infants based on spontaneous movement recordings.

Derivation of Labels

  • Outcome labels were assigned from clinical follow-up diagnoses of CP, confirmed by qualified clinicians using standardized diagnostic criteria.

  • Infants without a CP diagnosis were labeled as “non-CP.”

  • Labeling was performed independently of the video data to ensure no information contamination.

Justification of Label Choice

  • Intended Use Alignment: Labels directly reflect the intended medical purpose of predicting CP, ensuring relevance.

  • Clinical Validity: Diagnoses were based on internationally recognized clinical standards, supporting reliability and consistency.

  • Regulatory Traceability: The definition of labels corresponds precisely to the medical condition described in the intended purpose statement, fulfilling MDR requirements for traceability between intended use, training data, and method development.
