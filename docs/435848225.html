Deep In Motion : 10.8: Analysis of Influences of Type and Location of Data Collection

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

The influence of type of data collected (video recordings of infant spontaneous movements) and the location of collection (13 hospitals across Belgium, India, Norway, and the United States) on data quality, representativeness, and validity have been evaluated.

Analysis

  • Type of Data (Video Recordings)

    • Provides objective, non-invasive capture of motor function during the critical developmental window (9â€“18 weeks corrected age).

    • Standardized acquisition protocols (camera positioning, duration, lighting) were implemented across sites to minimize variability.

    • Risk: Technical heterogeneity in recording equipment could affect image quality.

    • Mitigation: Automated quality control and exclusion of substandard recordings.

  • Location of Data Collection (Multicenter, International)

    • Ensures diversity in demographic, ethnic, and socioeconomic characteristics, improving generalizability.

    • Variations in clinical practice and neonatal care pathways across countries may introduce site-specific differences.

    • Mitigation: Uniform eligibility criteria, centralized training, and monitoring across sites.

    • External validation confirmed that predictive performance remained consistent across centers.
