Deep In Motion : 10.5: Descriptive Statistics

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

Requirements

  • The manufacturer has described the collected data using descriptive statistics.

Summary

The clinical dataset is summarized using descriptive statistics to provide a transparent overview of the study population and data distribution.

Descriptive Statistics

  • Sample size: 557 infants.

  • Cohorts: 418 (75%) for method development; 139 (25%) for external validation.

  • Outcome prevalence: 84 infants (15.1%) diagnosed with cerebral palsy.

  • Demographics: Mean gestational age, birth weight, and sex distribution reported for both cohorts.

  • Data completeness: Proportion of recordings excluded due to technical failure or missing follow-up documented.

Justification

  • Descriptive statistics provide a clear characterization of the study population.

  • They enable assessment of representativeness, event prevalence, and comparability between development and external validation cohorts.

  • Transparency in reporting supports reproducibility and regulatory evaluation.
