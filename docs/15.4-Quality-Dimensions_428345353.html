Deep In Motion : 15.4 Quality Dimensions

Created by Felix Tempel, last updated by Daniel Groos on 20.08.2025

Requirements

  • The manufacturer has determined, documented and justified the quality dimensions on the basis of the intended use for which he wants to optimize the model.

Summary

The AI model is designed to support early screening for Cerebral Palsy (CP) in infants by analyzing spontaneous movement patterns in 3–5-minute video recordings during the “fidgety” period (9–18 weeks corrected age). It is not a diagnostic tool.

Optimized Quality Dimensions:

  • Primary:

    • AUC (Area Under ROC Curve): for global discrimination performance.

    • Specificity at 94.1%: prioritizing detection of at-risk infants with minimal false negatives.

  • Secondary:

    • PPV/NPV: based on estimated population prevalence (~15%).

    • Cross-site robustness: performance consistency across participating sites (Belgium, India, Norway, USA).


  • Threshold value:
    • The reported performance is based on a threshold value of ~0.35; the other threshold values are shown in the table below (Groos et al.).

Justification:
High sensitivity is critical due to the model’s triage use case. False negatives (missed referrals) pose a higher clinical risk than false positives.

These metrics were selected in consultation with domain experts in pediatric neurology and reflect expected clinical decision thresholds.
