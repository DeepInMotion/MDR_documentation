Regulatory references

11.1

In the case of "Supervised Learning", the manufacturer has derived the labels from the intended use with which the training data are provided and justified this choice.



11.2 (Lars review)

The manufacturer has defined a procedure for labeling in "Supervised Learning", if labels were not yet present in the data.


ISO 13485, 4.1

11.3 (Lars review)

This procedure specifies quantitative classification criteria for labeling. The selection of these criteria has been justified by the manufacturer12.

If the "Ground Truth” is not selected13, because it is too expensive or invasive, this must also be justified.


11.4 (Lars review)

This procedure specifies the requirements for the number, training and competency for the people responsible for labeling.


ISO 13485, 6.2 and 7.3.2, FDA 21 CFR part 820.25

11.5 (Lars input)

This procedure sets forth how the competencies of the persons responsible for labeling is tested.

This can be done by the labeling of selected data sets.


11.6 (Lars input)

This procedure sets forth how the persons responsible for labeling are trained and how the success of this training is evaluated.



11.7 (Lars input)

This procedure sets forth how the correctness of the label is systematically reviewed. The selection of this justification has been documented by the manufacturer.

The manufacturer can provide identical data sets of multiple persons and assess the consistency of the results.


11.8 (Lars input)

This procedure sets forth, how the monitoring occurs, that the persons responsible for labeling are continually fit and willing to perform the labeling 14.

This can be done with datasets with already known labels that are inserted unnoticed by the person during labeling.
