Regulatory references

10.1

The manufacturer has specified the number of data sets and given a justification why this is sufficient 6.

This concerns the data sets for training, validation and test.

ISO 13485, 7.3.7

10.2 (Lars review)

The manufacturer has characterized the inclusion and exclusion criteria of data by relevant attributes 7.

This includes documentation of data types, units and value range.

ISO 24028, XAVIER University "Building Explainability and Trust for AI in Healthcare"

10.3 (Lars review)

The manufacturer has specified technical inclusion and exclusion criteria for data8.



10.4 (Lars review)

The manufacturer has described the procedure by which it ensures that data sets that do not meet the inclusion criteria or should be excluded are actually excluded.

Procedure includes a software supported assessment. This software must be validated.


10.5

The manufacturer has described the collected data using descriptive statistics9.

The "Dataset Nutrition Label" is an recommended option.


10.6 (Lars review)

The manufacturer has justified where the data are collected and why these are representative for the target population. As reasonable, these have been compared to scientific publications and to registers.



10.7 (Lars review)

The manufacturer has listed and discussed factors that could cause a bias in the data.


ISO 24028 e.g. 10.5, DAISAM

10.8 (Lars review)

The manufacturer has analyzed which influences the type and location of data collection have on the data10.



10.9

The manufacturer has established a procedure to ensure that data protection requirements are met.

For example, the data is anonymized or pseudonymized before testing and training. The data protection officer should be involved in this.


10.10

The manufacturer has examined and excluded the possibility of a “label leakage”11.

This depends on the applied ML model is not a general best practice.


10.11

The manufacturer that uses surveys has justified the selection of the surveys, the time of survey and possibly the method for their assessment, in particular if no standardized survey exists.
