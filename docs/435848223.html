Deep In Motion : 10.7: Identification and Discussion of Potential Bias Factors

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

The clinical data collection and analysis processes were reviewed to identify potential sources of bias and to assess their impact on the validity of results.

Potential Bias Factors and Discussion

  • Selection Bias: Recruitment was limited to infants from hospitals participating in the study. While these represent diverse geographic regions, infants outside specialized care settings may be underrepresented.

  • Attrition Bias: A small number of infants were excluded due to missing follow-up or incomplete outcome confirmation. All exclusions were documented, and the impact on outcome prevalence was assessed.

  • Technical Quality Bias: Recordings with insufficient image quality were excluded. Although necessary for analysis, this may introduce bias if certain clinical sites or subgroups were more prone to technical failure.

  • Observer Bias: Clinical follow-up and diagnosis of cerebral palsy were conducted by experienced clinicians using standardized criteria, reducing—but not eliminating—the risk of subjective assessment.

  • Confounding Conditions: Infants with severe congenital anomalies were excluded to preserve diagnostic specificity. This may reduce generalizability to broader neonatal populations.

Mitigation Measures

  • Transparent documentation of inclusion/exclusion decisions.

  • Independent review of exclusions and outcome assessments.

  • Use of multicenter, international recruitment to minimize site-specific skew.

  • External validation to confirm generalizability of results.
