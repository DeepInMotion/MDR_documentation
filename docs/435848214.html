Deep In Motion : 10.4: Procedure for Enforcing Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

A documented procedure was established to ensure that all data used for model development and external validation comply with defined clinical and technical inclusion and exclusion criteria.

Procedure

  1. Initial Screening – Data are reviewed upon acquisition to confirm that basic clinical and technical requirements (age, corrected gestational week, recording quality) are met.

  2. Automated Quality Checks – Each video recording undergoes standardized technical validation (file integrity, minimum resolution, duration, and visibility checks).

  3. Clinical Verification – Clinical staff verify medical records and follow-up outcomes to confirm compliance with eligibility requirements.

  4. Dual Review – Any data flagged for potential exclusion are reviewed independently by at least two assessors.

  5. Exclusion Logging – Excluded data are documented with reason codes (e.g., “technical failure,” “insufficient follow-up” etc.).

  6. Audit Trail – All inclusion/exclusion decisions are recorded in the study database with timestamps and reviewer identifiers.

Justification

  • Ensures systematic removal of non-compliant data.

  • Provides traceability and transparency for regulatory review.

  • Prevents bias from inadvertent inclusion of unsuitable cases.
