Deep In Motion : 10.2: Characterization of Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

Requirements

  • The manufacturer has characterized the inclusion and exclusion criteria of data by relevant attributes.

Summary

Clear inclusion and exclusion criteria were defined to ensure that the clinical data used for method development and external validation are appropriate, representative, and relevant to the intended use population.

Inclusion Criteria

  • Infants aged 9â€“18 weeks corrected age at time of recording.

  • Clinically stable at time of assessment.

  • Availability of high-quality video recordings of spontaneous movements suitable for automated analysis.

  • Consent obtained from parents or legal guardians.

Exclusion Criteria

  • Infants with severe congenital anomalies or conditions unrelated to cerebral palsy likely to affect motor behavior.

  • Video recordings of insufficient technical quality (e.g., poor visibility, excessive artifacts).

  • Incomplete clinical follow-up preventing confirmation of cerebral palsy outcome status.

Justification

  • Age range ensures recordings correspond to the clinically recognized period where general movement assessment is valid.

  • Exclusion of infants with confounding conditions preserves the specificity of outcome attribution.

  • Technical quality requirements ensure the data are fit for automated algorithmic analysis.

  • Ensuring confirmed outcome status maintains integrity of diagnostic accuracy evaluation.
