Deep In Motion : 11.4: Requirements for Personnel Responsible for Labeling

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

Requirements

  • This procedure specifies the requirements for the number, training and competency for the people responsible for labeling.

Summary

Requirements for the number, training, and competency of personnel responsible for assigning outcome labels (cerebral palsy vs non-cerebral palsy) in supervised learning are specified.

Details

AspectSpecification
Number of ReviewersEach outcome must be confirmed by at least two independent clinicians. Discrepancies are resolved by consensus or, if needed, a third senior clinician.
QualificationsPersonnel responsible for outcome labeling must be licensed pediatric neurologists or developmental specialists with clinical experience in diagnosing cerebral palsy (CP).
Training Requirements

Reviewers must be trained in:

  • Standardized CP diagnostic criteria (including subtype classification and severity assessment).
  • Use of structured follow-up data for reliable diagnosis (e.g., assessments at â‰¥24 months corrected age).
Competency VerificationPrior to participation, reviewers must demonstrate competency by showing agreement with a reference set of previously adjudicated CP outcome cases.
Audit and DocumentationAll labeling decisions are logged with reviewer identifiers, diagnostic evidence, and final consensus status, ensuring a traceable audit trail.

Justification

  • Reliability: Dual independent review with consensus adjudication ensures consistent and reproducible labeling of CP outcomes.

  • Clinical Validity: Use of qualified clinicians with CP diagnostic expertise guarantees that labels reflect gold-standard medical practice.

  • Regulatory Compliance: Traceable, competency-verified labeling personnel align with MDR requirements for demonstrating that outcome data are accurate, reliable, and clinically justified.
