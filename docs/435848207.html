Deep In Motion : 10.3: Technical Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

Created by Daniel Groos, last updated on 19.08.2025

Technical requirements are defined to ensure only data of sufficient quality are used for method development and external validation.

Technical Inclusion Criteria

  • Video recordings with adequate resolution, frame rate, and lighting to capture spontaneous movements.

  • Recordings of sufficient duration to permit automated movement analysis.

  • Files in supported formats compatible with the analysis pipeline.

Technical Exclusion Criteria

  • Recordings with motion artifacts, obstructions, or poor visibility of the infant.

  • Corrupted or incomplete files (e.g., missing frames, interrupted recordings).

  • Data not conforming to standardized acquisition protocols across sites.

Justification

  • Ensures reliability of automated analysis and consistency across sites.

  • Prevents bias from degraded or unusable recordings.

  • Maintains reproducibility and integrity of validation outcomes.
